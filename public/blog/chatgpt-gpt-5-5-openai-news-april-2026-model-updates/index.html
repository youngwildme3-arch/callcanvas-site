What's New in ChatGPT and OpenAI This Month? April 2026 Model Updates, GPT-5.5 Features, and Ecosystem Changes

April 30, 2026 7 min read By the CallCanvas Team

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, 2026, just six weeks after GPT-5.4, marking the fastest model iteration cadence in the company's history. The launch came with something unexpected: API access was initially withheld for "different safeguards," ChatGPT now has more than 900 million weekly active users (up from 300 million in December 2024), and OpenAI quietly projects paid users will jump from 47 million to 122 million by year-end through a new $8 monthly plan. This isn't just a model update. It's a pivot in how the company prices, deploys, and monetizes AI.

GPT-5.5 Arrives With a Six-Week Turnaround and Efficiency Focus

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, 2026, calling it the company's "smartest and most intuitive to use model yet." The model launched to paid subscribers in ChatGPT and Codex, with API access following on April 24. What stands out isn't just the capabilities but the timeline: GPT-5.5 shipped only six weeks after GPT-5.4 (March 5), and followed GPT-5.2 (December 2025) by less than five months. OpenAI is now releasing flagship models on a sub-two-month rhythm.

GPT-5.5 is priced at $5 per million input tokens and $30 per million output tokens in the API, with GPT-5.5 Pro at $30 input and $180 output. That's higher than GPT-5.4 ($2.50/$15), but OpenAI says GPT-5.5 uses "significantly fewer tokens to complete the same Codex tasks," making it more token-efficient despite the higher per-token cost. The model supports a 1 million token context window, with prompts over 272K tokens priced at 2x input and 1.5x output.

According to OpenAI president Greg Brockman, the model is "a big step towards more agentic and intuitive computing," with particular strength in agentic coding, computer use, knowledge work, and scientific research. Benchmarks show GPT-5.5 scoring 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 and 51.7% on FrontierMath problems (difficulty 1-3), though an independent Tom's Guide test found GPT-5.5 lost to Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 in all seven comparison categories. OpenAI also reported that 4 million users are now active on Codex, with 9 million paying business users across ChatGPT.

What's Actually Changing: Pricing, Users, and the Shift to Volume

The biggest story in April wasn't a model release. It was a strategic shift revealed in internal projections: OpenAI expects its paid ChatGPT user base to grow from around 47 million in 2025 to about 122 million by the end of 2026, driven by a new $8 per month plan that could bring in over 100 million users. The $20 ChatGPT Plus tier is projected to see a sharp decline as users downgrade or new subscribers choose affordability over features.

This represents a fundamental change. OpenAI is moving from a high-margin, low-volume model to high-volume, lower-cost monetization. Internal estimates suggest average revenue per user will drop from about $23 per month to under $12. To compensate, OpenAI is rapidly building an advertising business. The company's ad pilot generated over $100 million in annualized revenue within weeks of launch, with hundreds of advertisers participating. OpenAI projects around $2.4 to $2.5 billion in ad revenue in 2026, with expectations that this could grow to almost $100 billion annually by 2030.

OpenAI also reduced ChatGPT Business seat pricing by $5 per month in April, and the company hit $2 billion in monthly revenue as of March 2026 (up from $20 billion annualized in 2025). ChatGPT now has over 900 million weekly active users globally, 50 million paid subscribers, and 7 million enterprise seats deployed. The projected subscriber jump to 122 million would more than double the paid base in under nine months, a pace driven entirely by price, not features.

New Features Beyond Models: Fast Answers and ChatGPT for Clinicians

OpenAI rolled out Fast Answers in April, a new mode that delivers quicker responses to common information-seeking questions with "high-confidence, in-depth replies." Fast Answers works globally on web, iOS, and Android, skips past chat history and memory, and can be turned off in Personalization settings. The feature is available to both logged-in and logged-out users across all plans, indicating OpenAI is optimizing for query speed and reducing compute on straightforward questions.

On April 30, OpenAI launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a free version for verified U.S. clinicians. It appears as a separate workspace under the same login and is designed to support real clinical work at the time of care, including evidence review, documentation, and medical research. Features include trusted clinical search, citations, reusable skills, deep research across medical literature, and support for earning CME (continuing medical education) credits on eligible activities. The product targets care-time workflows, not consumer health queries, and requires verification.

OpenAI also shipped improvements to model selection in the composer (making it easier to switch models before sending a message), moved thinking effort controls into the model picker, and added Advanced Account Security features on April 30. ChatGPT Images 2.0 launched April 21 with native reasoning, 2K resolution, multi-image consistency, and a December 2025 knowledge cutoff. DALL-E 2 and DALL-E 3 are being retired on May 12, 2026.

Ecosystem Expansion: AWS, Microsoft, and the Super App Strategy

OpenAI announced a major AWS partnership on April 28. GPT-5.5 and other frontier models are now available on Amazon Bedrock, Codex is coming to AWS, and Bedrock Managed Agents powered by OpenAI give enterprises new ways to build and deploy AI in trusted AWS environments with the security, governance, and workflows they already use. The partnership launched in limited preview and positions OpenAI to reach enterprises that prefer to stay within AWS infrastructure.

On April 27, OpenAI announced "the next phase" of its Microsoft partnership, though details were sparse. The company also released Symphony, an open-source specification for orchestration, on April 27, and published its "Our principles" document on April 26, describing its belief that AI can significantly improve society while also concentrating power if not handled carefully. The principles document lays out democratization, empowerment, and universal prosperity as core tenets.

Greg Brockman said GPT-5.5 brings OpenAI "one step closer" to its "super app" vision: combining ChatGPT, Codex, and AI browser into one unified service. OpenAI chief scientist Jakub Pachocki told press that the company sees "pretty significant improvements in the short term, extremely significant improvements in the medium term," signaling that the current release cadence is likely to continue. OpenAI has released five GPT-5.x models in seven months (GPT-5.1 in November, 5.2 in December, 5.3 in February, 5.4 in March, 5.5 in April), and GPT-5.1 models were officially deprecated on March 11, 2026.

FAQ

Is GPT-5 released yet, or is GPT-5.5 something different?

GPT-5 is the series name. OpenAI released GPT-5.1 in late 2025, then iterated with GPT-5.2, 5.3, 5.4, and 5.5 through April 2026. There is no single "GPT-5" model; the flagship is now GPT-5.5.

How much does ChatGPT cost in 2026?

ChatGPT Free remains available. ChatGPT Plus is $20/month. ChatGPT Pro is $200/month. ChatGPT Team is $25/user/month (annual) or $30/month (monthly). ChatGPT Business pricing dropped by $5/month per seat in April. A new $8/month tier is projected but not yet public.

Can I use GPT-5.5 for free?

No. GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro are only available to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users in ChatGPT and Codex. Free users get access to older models. GPT-5.5 Thinking is also restricted to paid plans.

What is the difference between GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro?

GPT-5.5 Pro offers higher accuracy and is designed for more demanding professional tasks. It's available only to Pro, Business, and Enterprise users. API pricing is $30 input / $180 output per million tokens, compared to $5 / $30 for standard GPT-5.5.

Did OpenAI really withhold API access at launch?

Yes. OpenAI released GPT-5.5 to ChatGPT and Codex users on April 23 but withheld API access until April 24, citing the need for "different safeguards" for API deployments. The API launched the next day with additional safety measures described in an updated system card.

How many people use ChatGPT now?

ChatGPT has more than 900 million weekly active users as of February 2026, up from 800 million in December 2025 and 400 million in February 2025. OpenAI reports over 50 million paying subscribers and 7 million enterprise seats.
